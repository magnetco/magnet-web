# METHOD.md

Magnet's complete offer architecture and methodology.

This document defines what we do, how we do it, and in what order. Use it when writing service pages, methodology content, or describing capabilities.

For strategic context, see [`STRATEGY.md`](STRATEGY.md). For voice and tone, see [`BRAND.md`](BRAND.md).

---

## Overview

**Foundation → Activation → Acceleration → Retention**

Each phase builds on the last. No phase can be skipped without consequences.

| Phase | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| Foundation | Build the strategic, narrative, and technical infrastructure that all growth depends on |
| Activation | Turn on demand to attract qualified traffic into the system |
| Acceleration | Improve acquisition efficiency, strengthen conversion, and increase revenue throughput |
| Retention | Extend lifetime value, reduce churn, and turn customers into advocates |

# 1. Foundation

Build the strategic, narrative, and technical infrastructure that all growth depends on.

## 1.1 Brand Architecture

Positioning → Narrative → Identity → Visual/Verbal System

### 1.1.1 Discovery

Research market, competitors, and audience motivations.

1. Gather internal materials (current website, decks, sales scripts, product documentation)
2. Identify direct and indirect competitors and collect their public messaging and positioning
3. Interview internal stakeholders to understand goals, audiences, and perceived value
4. Interview or survey customers/prospects to learn their motivations, needs, and buying triggers
5. Analyze competitive patterns, gaps, and opportunities
6. Summarize insights into a concise findings document

### 1.1.2 Positioning Definition

Determine the company's category, differentiation, and value drivers.

1. Define the market category the brand should be known for
2. List 3–5 differentiators backed by real capabilities or outcomes
3. Identify primary value drivers (speed, efficiency, revenue, risk reduction, quality)
4. Draft several positioning statement options
5. Evaluate each option for clarity, distinctiveness, credibility, and strategic fit
6. Select a final positioning statement and document the rationale

### 1.1.3 Narrative Development

Write the core story, value propositions, and proof structure.

1. Define the customer's "before" and "after" states in clear language
2. Write a problem → solution → outcome storyline
3. Draft 3–5 value propositions aligned to ICP motivations and proof points
4. List common objections and write concise responses
5. Assemble a unified narrative document
6. Review with leadership and sales, then refine based on feedback

### 1.1.4 Identity Design

Create visual language: logo, color, typography, imagery style.

1. Define 3–5 visual personality keywords aligned to the positioning
2. Build a moodboard that expresses these attributes
3. Create or refine logo concepts
4. Establish colors, typography, spacing, and imagery rules
5. Produce example applications (homepage hero, social tile, ad)
6. Finalize the identity system and prepare exportable assets

### 1.1.5 Systemization

Build brand guidelines and usage rules across channels.

1. Consolidate narrative, identity, and messaging elements in one location
2. Define rules for logo, color, typography, imagery, and tone
3. Create reusable templates (e.g., deck, one-pager, case study, social post)
4. Document adaptation guidelines for web, ads, email, and product
5. Publish the full brand system and train relevant team members

## 1.2 Messaging System

ICP Insights → Messaging Hierarchy → Page/Channel-Level Messaging

### 1.2.1 ICP Research

Gather behavioral, motivational, and contextual insights from ideal customers.

1. Define 2–4 Ideal Customer Profiles with role, industry, stage, and context
2. Interview or survey ICP representatives to understand pains, goals, and evaluation criteria
3. Review sales conversations to capture authentic buyer language
4. Identify patterns in motivations, objections, and desired outcomes
5. Summarize each ICP in a one-page profile

### 1.2.2 Message Architecture

Define value props, pains, benefits, RTBs, and tone.

1. List primary pains and desired benefits for each ICP
2. Translate pains into clear benefit-driven statements
3. Pair each benefit with a supporting proof point
4. Define tone of voice attributes aligned with brand positioning
5. Compile all elements into a structured messaging matrix

### 1.2.3 Framework Creation

Build headline formulas, offer structures, and objection-handling.

1. Write 5–10 headline formulas combining pain, value, and proof
2. Draft 2–3 offer framings that clearly communicate the solution and outcome
3. List common objections and write concise counterpoints
4. Convert these into modular messaging blocks for reuse across channels
5. Validate internally and refine based on feedback

### 1.2.4 Channel Adaptation

Convert messaging for web, ads, email, social, and product.

1. Identify requirements and constraints of each major channel
2. Translate core messaging into channel-specific examples
3. Write short, medium, and long variants of key messages
4. Ensure consistency across touchpoints without losing nuance
5. Store messaging assets in a shared repository

### 1.2.5 Validation

Review messaging against internal feedback and real-world performance.

1. Share messaging with sales, leadership, and product teams
2. Identify unclear, unproven, or misaligned statements
3. Test messages via ads, email subject lines, or landing page variants
4. Analyze performance signals (engagement, conversion, qualitative response)
5. Update the messaging system to reflect lessons learned

## 1.3 Digital Experience

UX → UI → Implementation → Optimization

### 1.3.1 UX Architecture

Define user flows, information architecture, and wireframes.

1. Identify primary user types and their top tasks
2. Map key user journeys from entry to conversion
3. Create a sitemap that supports these journeys with minimal friction
4. Produce low-fidelity wireframes for essential pages
5. Review flows and adjust based on user or stakeholder feedback

### 1.3.2 UI Design

Apply the brand system to layouts, components, and states.

1. Convert wireframes into high-fidelity mockups using the brand identity
2. Define reusable components and layout rules
3. Design responsive states for all breakpoints
4. Ensure accessibility basics (contrast, type size, interaction states)
5. Prepare a lightweight design system for implementation

### 1.3.3 Development

Build responsive, high-performance screens in the chosen framework.

1. Select the appropriate tech stack (Webflow, React/Next.js, CMS)
2. Set up the project with consistent naming and structure
3. Implement components and layouts accurately
4. Connect CMS collections, forms, and dynamic data
5. Optimize for performance and stability

### 1.3.4 Integration

Connect analytics, CRM, forms, and marketing tools.

1. Identify required integrations (analytics, CRM, email, pixels)
2. Implement tracking scripts and tag structures
3. Configure all forms to push data into the correct systems
4. Test end-to-end data flow to ensure accuracy
5. Document integration logic for future maintenance

### 1.3.5 QA & Refinement

Test for correctness, performance, accessibility, and polish.

1. Test all key journeys across browsers and devices
2. Fix layout issues, broken links, and content inconsistencies
3. Improve performance through optimization techniques
4. Validate accessibility fundamentals
5. Gather internal feedback and implement refinements

## 1.4 Conversion Architecture

Offer Pathways → Funnel Structure → Tracking Layer → Measurement

### 1.4.1 Offer Mapping

Identify conversion goals and corresponding offers/actions.

1. List core business goals (demos, signups, purchases, calls)
2. Define the offers associated with each conversion
3. Assign each offer to appropriate ICPs and channels
4. Identify primary and secondary CTAs for key pages
5. Prioritize offers by projected impact

### 1.4.2 Funnel Path Design

Build journey maps for TOFU/MOFU/BOFU flows.

1. Define each funnel stage in operational terms
2. Map common entry points into the funnel
3. Create visual pathways from first touch to conversion
4. Identify friction points and opportunities for support content
5. Document the funnel in a clear, shareable format

### 1.4.3 Tracking Implementation

Add events, goals, UTM standards, and pixel logic.

1. Create a structured tracking plan with event names and triggers
2. Implement events using GTM or custom code
3. Configure conversion goals inside analytics platforms
4. Standardize UTM naming conventions
5. Test event accuracy and data consistency

### 1.4.4 Measurement Setup

Define KPIs, dashboards, segments, and benchmarks.

1. Identify KPIs for acquisition, engagement, conversion, and revenue
2. Build dashboards showing performance by channel and funnel stage
3. Establish benchmarks based on historical or industry data
4. Determine reporting cadence and ownership
5. Align stakeholders on interpretation of metrics

### 1.4.5 Review & Calibration

Ensure funnels and tracking align with business goals.

1. Perform a full-funnel walkthrough during live tracking
2. Identify missing, incorrect, or redundant tracking logic
3. Correct funnel pathways or event structures as needed
4. Review dashboards with stakeholders and gather feedback
5. Finalize the measurement framework for ongoing use

Tracking → Tagging → Dashboards → Baselines

### 1.5.3 Dashboard Creation

Build clear reporting for traffic, conversions, and spend.

2. Select a dashboard tool
3. Connect relevant data sources
4. Build visualizations aligned to funnel stages
5. Validate accuracy across data sources

### 1.5.4 Baseline Analysis

Establish initial performance metrics.

1. Pull 3–12 months of historical data when available
2. Calculate averages and distributions for core KPIs
3. Identify early strengths and weaknesses
4. Produce a short baseline report
5. Use baselines to set improvement targets

### 1.5.5 Governance

Maintain data hygiene and consistent naming conventions.

1. Document event names, UTM standards, and dashboard definitions
2. Assign ownership for analytics maintenance
3. Schedule regular checks for broken events or inconsistencies
4. Train relevant team members on reading and interpreting data
5. Update documentation when changes are made so nothing drifts

# 2. Activation

Turn on demand to attract qualified traffic into the system.

## 2.1 Paid Media

Predictable acquisition through targeted advertising and continuous optimization.

### 2.1.1 Channel Strategy

Determine which paid platforms to use and how they support the funnel.

1. Identify ICPs and the platforms where they actively spend attention
2. Map each platform to funnel stages (prospecting, retargeting, high intent)
3. Define campaign objectives (traffic, leads, conversions, sales)
4. Outline audience segments, budgets, and pacing
5. Select primary KPIs for each platform and campaign type

### 2.1.2 Creative Development

Produce compelling ad assets tailored to audience and channel.

1. Define campaign message and creative concept based on ICP motivations
2. Produce variations of static, video, and motion assets
3. Adapt creative for platform-specific dimensions and best practices
4. Incorporate strong CTAs aligned with offer and funnel stage
5. Prepare multiple creative versions for testing

### 2.1.3 Campaign Build

Structure and set up campaigns across platforms.

1. Create campaigns with clear naming conventions
2. Build ad groups or ad sets with targeted criteria and budget allocations
3. Upload creative assets and align them to the correct audiences
4. Configure tracking parameters (UTMs, conversion events)
5. Review all configurations before launching

### 2.1.4 Launch & Optimization

Run campaigns, test variables, and improve performance.

1. Launch campaigns and verify that tracking fires correctly
2. Monitor early performance for cost, click-through, and engagement indicators
3. Test creative versions, audiences, placements, and bidding strategies
4. Pause underperforming assets and move budget to top performers
5. Produce weekly insights summarizing performance and next steps

### 2.1.5 Scaling

Increase spend on efficient campaigns while maintaining performance.

1. Identify top-performing creative and audience patterns
2. Increase budgets gradually to maintain stability
3. Expand winning campaigns into new audiences or geographies
4. Introduce new creative themes informed by prior insights
5. Monitor efficiency metrics and adjust scaling pace accordingly

# 3. Acceleration

Improve acquisition efficiency, strengthen conversion, and increase revenue throughput.

## 3.1 Offers & Packaging

Simplify buying, strengthen differentiation, and increase close rates.

### 3.1.1 Insight Gathering

Understand customer motivations and the internal value story.

1. Review customer interviews, sales calls, and feedback surveys
2. Identify top pains, desired outcomes, and buying triggers
3. Document objections and questions that appear consistently
4. Gather internal perspectives on strengths, gaps, and key capabilities
5. Summarize insights into a clear opportunity statement

### 3.1.2 Offer Structuring

Define the value, scope, and outcomes of each offer.

1. Outline the primary problem each offer solves
2. List deliverables and explain their strategic value
3. Clarify differentiators and proprietary methods
4. Define expected outcomes and success metrics
5. Decide on 1–3 versions or tiers of the offer

### 3.1.3 Pricing Strategy

Establish pricing that aligns with market value and business goals.

1. Research competitor pricing and market expectations
2. Model pricing scenarios based on cost, margins, and value delivered
3. Define pricing tiers or add-ons if relevant
4. Anticipate common pricing objections and prepare responses
5. Document a final pricing structure with rationale

### 3.1.4 Presentation Assets

Build materials that communicate the offer clearly and persuasively.

1. Create a one-page summary of each offer
2. Build a slide deck that communicates narrative and outcomes
3. Prepare website content for the offer page
4. Produce visual examples or case studies that reinforce credibility
5. Store assets in a shared library for sales use

### 3.1.5 Validation

Test the offer in real conversations and refine it.

1. Present the offer to internal teams for feedback
2. Pilot the offer with a small group of prospects
3. Note which parts resonate most and where confusion appears
4. Adjust structure, language, or pricing as needed
5. Publish the finalized offer for full usage

# 4. Retention

Extend lifetime value, reduce churn, and turn customers into advocates.

## 4.1 Lifecycle Email

Use automated, behavior-driven communication to onboard, engage, expand, and reactivate customers.

### 4.1.1 Onboarding Buildout

Guide new customers to their first moment of value.

1. Identify the ideal "first value" milestone for the customer
2. Map the steps required to reach that milestone
3. Write onboarding emails that clearly explain each step
4. Add supportive resources (guides, videos, FAQs)
5. Connect triggers so emails send immediately after key actions

### 4.1.2 Engagement Sequences

Maintain momentum through education and reinforcement.

1. Identify behaviors that indicate partial activation
2. Draft educational emails that address common gaps or questions
3. Schedule content based on user behavior, not time alone
4. Add CTAs that deepen product or service usage
5. Test open, click, and engagement patterns monthly

### 4.1.3 Expansion Flows

Introduce upgrades, add-ons, and complementary services.

1. Identify natural upgrade points in the customer journey
2. Define offers that deliver additional value at those moments
3. Write sequences explaining benefits and use cases
4. Segment customers based on readiness signals
5. Monitor conversion rates and refine messaging

### 4.1.4 Reactivation

Win back inactive or at-risk customers.

1. Identify inactivity triggers (time-based or behavior-based)
2. Segment users by degree of inactivity
3. Write personalized messages acknowledging their drop-off
4. Provide incentives, updates, or value-driven reasons to return
5. Track reactivation rates and adjust offers

### 4.1.5 Monthly Optimization

Improve lifecycle performance through continuous refinement.

1. Review open, click, and conversion rates
2. Identify sequences with low engagement or high friction
3. Rewrite or shorten messages where necessary
4. Test subject lines, CTAs, and content order
5. Document improvements and performance trends

## 4.5 Predictive Retention Intelligence

Use data, segmentation, and predictive indicators to prevent churn, surface growth opportunities, and drive more intelligent retention decisions.

### 4.5.1 Segmentation Framework

Organize customers into actionable groups based on behavior, value, and risk.

1. Define segmentation logic (usage, lifecycle stage, value, intent)
2. Assign customers to segments automatically through analytics
3. Map triggers that determine when users move between segments
4. Identify segment-specific risks and opportunities
5. Sync segments with CRM, email, and reporting tools

### 4.5.2 Health Scoring

Measure the strength of each customer relationship with a simple, predictive score.

1. Identify key indicators of customer satisfaction and risk
2. Assign weighted scoring to each behavior or signal
3. Calculate health scores weekly or monthly
4. Flag customers dropping below thresholds
5. Integrate health scores into account reviews and outreach

### 4.5.3 Churn Prediction

Forecast which customers are most likely to churn before they disengage.

1. Analyze historic churn to identify early signals
2. Build a rule-based or ML-driven prediction model
3. Label users as "healthy," "at-risk," or "critical"
4. Automate alerts to Customer Success or Lifecycle Email
5. Track accuracy and retrain or refine models quarterly

### 4.5.4 Proactive Retention Interventions

Execute targeted actions to prevent churn and increase satisfaction.

1. Define intervention playbooks for each risk category
2. Prepare tailored offers, support outreach, or content
3. Trigger interventions automatically based on risk level
4. Measure improvement in health score and retention
5. Adjust playbooks based on performance data

### 4.5.5 Predictive Revenue Expansion

Identify expansion-ready customers and surface timely upsell opportunities.

1. Identify behavioral indicators of upgrade readiness
2. Build predictive scoring for expansion likelihood
3. Surface high-potential accounts in CRM
4. Trigger tailored expansion messaging or Success outreach
5. Measure expansion rate and refine scoring
